About

I have been a tech enthusiast since my first introduction to computers on the Mac Power PC in the early 90’s. My first hook into UNIX/Linux was around the mid 90’s when I discovered terminals could be made transparent.

I studied Chemistry in university,but I was always pulled towards the computational side and my PhD and Post-Doctoral research were in computational biology. I worked on large scale simulations of proteins to predict stability and to better understand drug interactions. My work included modification of a GROMACS for tailor made calculations and lots of Python and Shell application development for data analysis of large (by early 2000’s standards) data sets.

Most recently, I have taken an interest in the cross section of Hardware and Security. This page is a personal record of projects in this area.

Project Areas

  • Low-level data handling
  • Device modification and interaction
  • Network security
